Understanding Climate Change Through Probability

This project applies probability and statistical methods to analyze real-world climate change data. By visualizing key trends—such as global temperature rise, carbon dioxide emissions, and sea level changes—I explored how statistical tools can uncover the patterns driving climate change.

Using bar graphs, line charts, pie charts, and histograms, I identified distributions, calculated probabilities, and made predictions based on historical and current climate data. This helped me understand how we can model environmental uncertainties and support data-driven decisions about our planet's future.

Scroll through the sections to explore how probability deepens our understanding of climate change.

Linear Regression
